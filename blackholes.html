The Mysterious World of Black Holes

Explore the most enigmatic objects in the universe where gravity is so strong that nothing, not even light, can escape.

Types of Black Holes

Stellar Black Holes

Formed when massive stars collapse at the end of their life cycle. Typically 5 to 100 times the mass of our Sun.

  • Created by supernova explosions
  • Common throughout galaxies
  • Strong gravitational pull

Supermassive Black Holes

Monsters at the center of galaxies, millions to billions times the Sun's mass. Our Milky Way has Sagittarius A*.

  • Found in galactic centers
  • Power quasars and active galaxies
  • Formation still mysterious

Intermediate Black Holes

The "missing link" between stellar and supermassive black holes, with hundreds to thousands of solar masses.

  • Rare and hard to detect
  • May form through mergers
  • Important for understanding BH growth

Mind-Blowing Black Hole Facts

Time Dilation

Time runs slower near a black hole due to extreme gravity. An hour near the event horizon could be years elsewhere.

Spaghettification

The tidal forces stretch objects into long, thin shapes like spaghetti as they fall in.

Hawking Radiation

Black holes can slowly evaporate by emitting quantum particles at their event horizons.

First Image

The first black hole image (M87*) was captured in 2019 by the Event Horizon Telescope.

Black Hole Discovery Timeline

1783: John Michell's "Dark Stars"

First proposed the idea of massive objects whose gravity prevents light from escaping.

1915: Einstein's General Relativity

Provided the theoretical framework for understanding black holes.

1964: Cygnus X-1 Discovered

First strong black hole candidate found through X-ray emissions.

2019: First Black Hole Image

Event Horizon Telescope captured the shadow of M87*'s black hole.
